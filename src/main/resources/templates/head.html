Dry Eyes

The eyes are among the most sensitive tissues in the body, making sufficient levels of Vitamin A and Vitamin D essential. A deficiency in these vitamins can weaken the stability of the tear film and increase inflammation, leading to symptoms of dry eyes. Vitamin A helps maintain the health of the eye's surface, while Vitamin D regulates inflammation and supports tear production, ensuring lubrication and protection of the eyes.

  1. Vitamin A

Role:
  • Maintains the health of the cornea and supports the function of the lacrimal glands.
  • Protects the mucous membranes and epithelial tissues of the eyes, preventing tear evaporation.

  2. Vitamin D

Role:
  • Reduces inflammation in the tear film and promotes tear production.
  • Regulates autoimmune responses in the eyes, alleviating inflammation associated with dry eye syndrome.
